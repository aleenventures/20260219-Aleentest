Who We Are

Seven decades of
literary courage.

Founded in 1952 by Edmund Carver and Heloise Morin-Taubert in a narrow office on West 44th Street, Meridian Press began as a passionate experiment: what if a publisher placed literary quality above all else? Over seventy years later, that founding conviction remains our compass.

We have published Pulitzer Prize winners and complete unknowns, established voices and brilliant debuts. What unites our catalogue is not genre but intention — books written with care, published with commitment, and built to last.

Today, Meridian Press remains proudly independent, employee-owned, and headquartered in New York City. We publish approximately forty titles per year, each one chosen because we believe the world needs it.

M
01 / Values

Literary Courage

We publish books that take risks. Stories that challenge, discomfort, and ultimately expand a reader's understanding of what it means to be human. We will never publish to trend.

02 / Values

Author Partnership

Our relationship with authors is built on mutual respect and transparency. We believe the author's vision leads — our editors are there to serve it, not supplant it.

03 / Values

Craft & Rigour

Every manuscript receives the full attention of our editorial team. We take the time a book requires, however long that may be. Great books are not made in a hurry.

04 / Values

Independent Voice

As an employee-owned press, our decisions are made by people who love books — not quarterly earnings targets. That independence is sacred to us.

What We're Looking

We Are Actively Acquiring

Literary fiction with a strong sense of place. Narrative non-fiction in the tradition of long-form journalism. Translated works from underrepresented languages. Debut novelists with an unmistakable voice. Cross-genre works that defy easy categorisation.

We Are Not Currently Acquiring

Genre romance, thrillers, or fantasy. Self-help and lifestyle titles. Children's or middle-grade fiction. Poetry collections. Screenplays or stage plays. Please note that unsolicited full manuscripts are not read — queries and proposals only.
